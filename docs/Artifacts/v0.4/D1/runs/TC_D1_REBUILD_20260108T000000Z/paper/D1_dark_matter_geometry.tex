\section{Question (model-scoped)}
Can the Tessaris photon algebra produce regions of curvature energy that exert gravitational influence
but are invisible to local field observables $(|\psi_1|,|\psi_2|)$, thereby acting as a dark-mass analogue?

\section{Method}
The simulation extends the wormhole-geometry style setup with a \emph{visible sector} and \emph{hidden sector}:
\[
(\psi_1,\psi_2)\;\rightarrow\;\text{visible sector},\qquad
(\psi_3,\psi_4)\;\rightarrow\;\text{hidden sector}.
\]
The hidden fields couple only through the curvature channel $\Lambda_t \kappa(x,y)$ and have no $\alpha$-term interaction.
Evolution equations:
\[
\dot{\psi}_i = i\hbar\nabla^2\psi_i - \alpha_i \psi_i + i\Lambda_i \kappa \psi_i,
\]
where $\alpha_i=\alpha$ for visible fields and $\alpha_i=0$ for hidden fields. All fields are initialized
with Gaussian curvature wells and evolved for $1600$ steps with $\Delta t=0.006$.

f_{\text{dark}} &= \frac{E_{\text{hid}}}{E_{\text{vis}} + E_{\text{hid}}}, \\
