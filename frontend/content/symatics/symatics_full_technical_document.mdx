# Symatics Technical Specification (Unified Draft)
**Version 0.2.2 (polished) | Generated: 2025-12-22**

---

## Abstract
Symatics is a wave-native algebra where *signatures* are first-class values and composition is phase-aware. Its core connective is a phase-parameterized interference operator **$\bowtie[\phi]$** whose generic behavior is not reducible to Boolean logic.

This specification defines: (i) a minimal semantic model as an evaluation into complex amplitude vectors, (ii) normalization axioms A1–A8 for mechanized rewriting, (iii) derived laws and a formal *Classical Boundary* (Boolean-like behavior only at **$\phi \in \{0,\pi\}$**), (iv) collapse semantics as a distribution with a default Born-rule bridge, and (v) a reproducible validation workflow and substrate mapping.

---

## 1. Motivation
Most formal systems begin with discrete tokens (numbers, bits) and approximate wave phenomena later. Symatics inverts this: it begins with measurable wave structure (amplitude and phase) and defines symbolic operators over it.

Design goals:

- **G1 Phase-aware composition:** express constructive vs. destructive behavior.
- **G2 Substrate portability:** same algebra maps to circuits/light/sound.
- **G3 Mechanized normalization:** deterministic rewriting + audit trail.
- **G4 Continuous-to-discrete bridge:** collapse from wave expressions to discrete outcomes.

## 2. Core Objects

### 2.1 Signatures
Atomic labels:

**$\Sigma = \{A,B,\dots\}$**

### 2.2 Wave model (minimal)
Each signature **$\sigma \in \Sigma$** may be interpreted as a phasor with amplitude and phase:

$$
\mathrm{Wave}(\sigma)=(\sigma,\alpha_\sigma,\varphi_\sigma)
\quad\text{where}\quad
\alpha_\sigma \in \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0},\;\varphi_\sigma \in [0,2\pi).
$$

### 2.3 Expressions
Expressions are trees over **$\Sigma$** using operator nodes (Section 3). Implementations may represent expressions as AST nodes or nested structures.

### 2.4 The annihilator $\bot$
**$\bot$** denotes the *null / annihilated expression*. Semantically, it is the zero amplitude-vector:

$$
\mathrm{Eval}(\bot)=\mathbf{0}\in\mathbb{C}^\Sigma.
$$

### 2.5 Distributions
$\mathrm{Dist}(\Sigma)$ denotes probability distributions over discrete outcomes $\sigma\in\Sigma$.

## 3. Syntax and Operators

### 3.1 Concrete syntax (illustrative)

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{Expr} ::= {}& \mathrm{Atom}
\;\mid\;(\mathrm{Expr}\oplus\mathrm{Expr})
\;\mid\;(\mathrm{Expr}\bowtie[\phi]\mathrm{Expr})
\;\mid\;(\mathrm{Expr}\leftrightarrow\mathrm{Expr})\\
&\mid\;(\circlearrowleft\,\mathrm{Expr})
\;\mid\;\nabla(\mathrm{Expr})
\;\mid\;(\mathrm{Action}\Rightarrow\mathrm{Expr})
\end{aligned}
$$

where $\phi\in\mathbb{R}$ is interpreted modulo $2\pi$.

### 3.2 Operator catalogue

| Symbol | Name | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $\oplus$ | Superpose | Linear combine; associative/commutative in base algebra; idempotence optional by law set. |
| $\ominus$ | Difference | Derived; used as the $\phi=\pi$ boundary form of $\bowtie[\phi]$. |
| $\bowtie[\phi]$ | Interfere | Primary phase-parameterized connective (beyond-Boolean). |
| $\leftrightarrow$ | Couple / Entangle | Constructor over paired args; stronger constraints may be added as extensions. |
| $\circlearrowleft$ | Resonate | Iterative reinforcement/decay; semantics may be substrate/implementation-specific. |
| $\nabla$ | Collapse / Measure | $\mathrm{Expr}\to \mathrm{Dist}(\Sigma)$; policy is configurable (Born-like default provided). |
| $\Rightarrow$ | Trigger | Side-effect bridge: symbolic $\to$ runtime action (kept out of algebraic rewrites). |

### 3.3 Boundary behavior
At $\phi=0$ and $\phi=\pi$, interference degenerates to familiar forms (A7/A8). This is the *Classical Boundary* where Boolean-like behavior can emerge.

## 4. Minimal Semantics (Evaluation to Complex Amplitudes)
This section gives the “ground truth” semantics that a physicist/CS reviewer can verify.

### 4.1 Evaluation function
Define an evaluation function mapping an expression $E$ to a complex amplitude vector over $\Sigma$:

$$
\mathrm{Eval}(E)\in\mathbb{C}^\Sigma,
\quad\text{where}\quad \mathrm{Eval}(E)(\sigma)\in\mathbb{C}
\text{ is the amplitude assigned to signature }\sigma.
$$

### 4.2 Atoms
For an atom $A\in\Sigma$:

$$
\mathrm{Eval}(A)(A)=\alpha_A\,e^{i\varphi_A},
\qquad
\mathrm{Eval}(A)(\sigma)=0\;\;\text{for }\sigma\neq A.
$$

### 4.3 Superposition and difference
For expressions $X,Y$:

$$
\mathrm{Eval}(X\oplus Y)=\mathrm{Eval}(X)+\mathrm{Eval}(Y),
\qquad
\mathrm{Eval}(X\ominus Y)=\mathrm{Eval}(X)-\mathrm{Eval}(Y).
$$

### 4.4 Interference connective $\bowtie[\phi]$
For expressions $X$ and $Y$ with relative phase shift $\phi$ applied to the *right* operand:

$$
\mathrm{Eval}\!\bigl(X\bowtie[\phi]Y\bigr)=\mathrm{Eval}(X)+e^{i\phi}\,\mathrm{Eval}(Y).
$$

Componentwise, for each $\sigma\in\Sigma$:

$$
\mathrm{Eval}\!\bigl(X\bowtie[\phi]Y\bigr)(\sigma)=\mathrm{Eval}(X)(\sigma)+e^{i\phi}\,\mathrm{Eval}(Y)(\sigma).
$$

### 4.5 Phase normalization
All phases are interpreted modulo $2\pi$ and normalized to $[0,2\pi)$:

$$
\mathrm{normalize\_phase}(\phi)=\bigl((\phi \bmod 2\pi)+2\pi\bigr)\bmod 2\pi.
$$

### 4.6 Substrate-agnostic core
This minimal semantics assumes linear superposition at the semantic layer. Substrate-specific effects (loss, dispersion, decoherence, drift) are modeled as additional operators or parameterized noise applied prior to evaluation/collapse.

## 5. Axioms and Rewrite Rules (A1–A8)
A1–A8 define the *normalization interface* for mechanized rewriting of $\bowtie[\phi]$ expressions. They are presented as rewrite schemas used by the rewriter until a fixed point is reached.

**The Golden Law of Resonance:**

$$
(X \bowtie[0] X)\to X
$$

*(Self-identity / constructive alignment)*

**The Law of Annihilation:**

$$
(X \bowtie[\pi] X)\to \bot
$$

*(Self-annihilation / destructive cancellation)*

| Id | Schema | Meaning |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **A1** | $(X\bowtie[0]X)\to X$ | Self-identity (constructive alignment). |
| **A2** | $(X\bowtie[\pi]X)\to \bot$ | Self-annihilation (destructive cancellation). |
| **A3** | $(X\bowtie[\phi]\bot)\to X$ | Right annihilator (using $\mathrm{Eval}(\bot)=0$). |
| **A4** | $(X\bowtie[\phi]Y)\to (Y\bowtie[-\phi]X)$ | Swap operands with phase inversion. |
| **A5** | $((X\bowtie[\phi]Y)\bowtie[\psi]Z)\to (X\bowtie[\phi+\psi](Y\bowtie[\psi]Z))$ | Right-associate; phase addition. |
| **A6** | $(X\bowtie[\phi](X\bowtie[-\phi]Y))\to Y$ | Cancellation. |
| **A7** | $(A\bowtie[0]B)\leftrightarrow (A\oplus B)$ | Boundary: $\phi=0$ reduces to superposition. |
| **A8** | $(A\bowtie[\pi]B)\leftrightarrow (A\ominus B)$ | Boundary: $\phi=\pi$ reduces to difference. |

**Complexity note (engineering).** In the reference normalization strategy, rewriting is performed with phase-canonicalization and associative flattening; empirically and by standard multiset/normal-form arguments, normalization can be implemented in **$O(n\log n)$** time in expression size $n$ (relative to tree depth and sorting/canonicalization steps), ensuring symbolic interference remains far cheaper than brute-force wave-equation simulation.

## 6. Theorems and the Classical Boundary
Theorems are validated by normalization equivalence checks and (optionally) proof-assistant audit. The key separation result is that generic phase behavior cannot be represented by Boolean connectives.

### 6.1 Theorem ledger (informal statements)

| Id | Statement (informal) |
| :---: | :--- |
| **T1** | $(A\bowtie[\phi]A)\leftrightarrow A \iff \phi=0$. |
| **T2** | $(A\bowtie[\phi]A)\leftrightarrow \bot \iff \phi=\pi$. |
| **T3** | $A\bowtie[\phi](A\bowtie[-\phi]B)\leftrightarrow B$. |
| **T4** | $((A\bowtie[\phi]B)\bowtie[\psi]C)\leftrightarrow (A\bowtie[\phi+\psi](B\bowtie[\psi]C))$. |
| **T5** | Distributivity fails for **$\phi\notin\{0,\pi\}$**. |
| **T6** | $X=A\bowtie[\phi]X$ has no solutions for **$\phi\notin\{0,\pi\}$**. |
| **T7** | (Classical Boundary) non-distributivity with classical conjunction (below). |

### 6.2 Theorem T7 (Classical Boundary / irreducibility)
**Notation.** In T7, $\land$ denotes *classical Boolean conjunction* used as an external gating/filtering operator for the separation argument (it is not required to be a primitive Symatics operator).

**Statement.** For **$\phi\notin\{0,\pi\}$**:

$$
\bigl((A\bowtie[\phi]B)\land C\bigr)\;\neq\;\bigl((A\land C)\bowtie[\phi](B\land C)\bigr).
$$

**Interpretation.** Boolean distributivity is not universal; it is a boundary case of wave behavior. At **$\phi\in\{0,\pi\}$**, the connective degenerates to classical-like behavior via A7/A8. For all other phases, the system is *beyond-Boolean*.

### 6.3 Irreducibility intuition
Boolean connectives are phase-insensitive. Since $\bowtie[\phi]$ varies continuously with $\phi$, replacing it by any fixed Boolean connective loses information. T7 captures this as a structural law failure (non-distributivity) for all non-trivial phases.

## 7. Collapse Semantics and the Born-Rule Bridge

### 7.1 Collapse as a distribution
Collapse maps expressions to distributions:

$$
\nabla:\mathrm{Expr}\to \mathrm{Dist}(\Sigma),
\qquad
\sum_{\sigma\in\Sigma} P(\sigma)=1.
$$

This specification is *policy-agnostic*: deterministic or stochastic collapse is permitted, but the policy must be declared.

### 7.2 Default policy: Born-like amplitude-squared normalization
Let $v=\mathrm{Eval}(E)\in\mathbb{C}^\Sigma$. Define:

$$
\mathrm{raw}(\sigma)=|v(\sigma)|^2,
\qquad
P(\sigma)=\frac{\mathrm{raw}(\sigma)}{\sum_{\tau\in\Sigma}\mathrm{raw}(\tau)}.
$$

This provides a clean bridge from continuous amplitude composition to discrete outcomes. (Extensions may apply decoherence/drift transforms to $v$ prior to squaring.)

### 7.3 Deterministic policy (example)
A deterministic collapse is permitted as a special case, e.g. $\sigma^\star=\arg\max_\sigma |v(\sigma)|^2$, with $P(\sigma^\star)=1$ and $0$ otherwise.

## 10. Worked Examples

### 10.1 Cancellation (T3)
Let $E:=A\bowtie[\phi]\bigl(A\bowtie[-\phi]B\bigr)$. Normalize using A6:

$$
A\bowtie[\phi]\bigl(A\bowtie[-\phi]B\bigr)\to B.
$$

### 10.2 Phase-swap symmetry (A4)

$$
A\bowtie[\phi]B \;\to\; B\bowtie[-\phi]A.
$$

This matches the phasor semantics: shifting relative phase from the right operand to the left requires sign inversion.

### 10.3 Interference as a phase gate (sketch)
Let basis signatures be $|0\rangle=A$, $|1\rangle=B$. Define $\mathrm{state}:=A\bowtie[\phi]B$ and $\mathrm{out}:=\nabla(\mathrm{state})$. With equal amplitudes, $\phi\approx 0$ biases toward constructive outcomes; $\phi\approx\pi$ biases toward cancellation.

### 10.4 Signature-based distance (expanded sketch)
Let $S_{\mathrm{ref}}$ be a calibrated fringe signature and measurement yields $S_{\mathrm{meas}}$. Define a metric $d$ over signatures (e.g. via phase-frequency correlation). Then distance becomes $d(S_{\mathrm{meas}},S_{\mathrm{ref}})$.

**Trigger semantics (clarification).** The operator $\Rightarrow$ is intentionally excluded from purely algebraic rewrites. It bridges normalized symbolic patterns to runtime actions (e.g. scheduling an intent, emitting an event, selecting a branch).
